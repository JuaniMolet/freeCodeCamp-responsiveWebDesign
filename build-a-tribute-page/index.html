Dr. Srinivasa Ramanujan

The man who taught infinity

Here's a timeline of Dr. Srinivasa Ramanujan's life:

  • 1887 - Born in Erode, Mysore, India.
  • 1889 - Moved to Kanchipuram, Chennai due to a smallpox outbreak.
  • 1892 - Enrolled in a local school, where a local constable ensured his attendance.
  • 1897 - Passed primary exams in English, Tamil, geography, and arithmetic with top scores.
  • 1904 - Developed Bernoulli numbers and calculated the Eulerâ€“Mascheroni constant to 15 decimal places.
  • 1906 - Failed the Fellow of Arts exam but continued independent research.
  • 1910 - Gained recognition in Madras's mathematical circles.
  • 1911 - Published "Some Properties of Bernoulli's Numbers" with important conjectures.
  • 1913 - Corresponded with G.H. Hardy, leading to global recognition.
  • 1914 - Traveled to England, impressing mathematicians like Hardy and Littlewood.
  • 1916 - Earned a Bachelor of Arts by Research for work on highly composite numbers.
  • 1917 - Elected to the London Mathematical Society.
  • 1918 - Elected a Fellow of the Royal Society and Trinity College, Cambridge.
  • 1919 - Diagnosed with tuberculosis and returned to India.
  • 1920 - Passed away at the age of 32, leaving behind significant mathematical work.
